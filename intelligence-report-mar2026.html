Key Insights — March 2026
Plain English summary of what's happening across both regions

🔥 March is the biggest month we've ever recorded — and it's not over yet

We've tracked 283 new planning applications in Brisbane and the Sunshine Coast this month — that's four times the normal volume from November/December. What this means practically: there are more property projects starting right now than at any point in the last year. For anyone in the property industry, this is the best possible time to be in front of new clients.

📍 Camp Hill, Hendra, and Ascot are this month's hottest Brisbane suburbs

These three suburbs have each had 5 or more new planning applications in the last 30 days — and Camp Hill's application rate tripled week on week. This isn't a random spike; it's a connected cluster of inner-city suburbs where homeowners and developers are clearly on the move. If you work in any of these areas, now is the time to be visible and proactive.

🌊 Maroochydore has been in the top 3 for 6 months straight

The Sunshine Coast isn't spiking — it's consistently producing. Maroochydore alone has had 17 planning applications in the last few months, with 10 of them being commercial projects. This is a market that rewards long-term relationships, not one-off deals. If you're not actively building your network there, someone else is.

🔗 We can now track a project from first application to completion

myFindr now links every application at the same address together — so when a property goes through subdivision, then operational works, then building work, you can see the whole journey. This means you'll know exactly where a project is in its life cycle and exactly when it's the right time for you to get involved — not too early, not too late.
